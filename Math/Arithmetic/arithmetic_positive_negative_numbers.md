
# Positive and Negative Numbers

## Description

Positive and negative numbers appear throughout mathematics, engineering,
electronics, programming, measurements, temperatures, voltage, current, and
coordinate systems.

This card explains how their signs behave during:

- addition;
- subtraction;
- multiplication;
- division.

---

## 1. Understanding Positive and Negative Numbers

A **positive number** is greater than zero:

$$+5$$

A **negative number** is less than zero:

$$-5$$

Zero is neither positive nor negative.

On a number line:

- positive numbers are to the right of zero;
- negative numbers are to the left of zero.

The sign tells us which side of zero the number is on. The number's
**magnitude** tells us its distance from zero.

For example, (+7) and (-7) have the same magnitude, but opposite signs.

$$\text{Opposite of } -7 = +7$$

$$\text{Opposite of } +7 = -7$$

---

## 2. Adding Positive and Negative Numbers

### Numbers With the Same Sign

When both numbers have the same sign:

1. Add their magnitudes.
2. Keep their common sign.

Positive plus positive gives a positive result:

$$(+4)+(+3)=+7$$

Negative plus negative gives a negative result:

$$(-4)+(-3)=-7$$

> Adding two negative numbers moves further below zero.

### Numbers With Different Signs

When the numbers have different signs:

1. Subtract the smaller magnitude from the larger magnitude.
2. Keep the sign of the number with the larger magnitude.

In this example, (8) has the larger magnitude, so the result is positive:

$$8+(-3)=5$$

In this example, (-8) has the larger magnitude, so the result is negative:

$$3+(-8)=-5$$

If both numbers have the same magnitude but opposite signs, the result is zero:

$$6+(-6)=0$$

---

## 3. Subtracting Positive and Negative Numbers

Subtraction can be rewritten as **addition of the opposite**:

$$a-b=a+(-b)$$

This means that you change the subtraction sign to addition and reverse the
sign of the number being subtracted.

### Subtracting a Positive Number

Subtracting a positive number moves the value towards the negative direction:

$$7-3=7+(-3)=4$$

Starting with a negative number works the same way:

$$-4-3=-4+(-3)=-7$$

### Subtracting a Negative Number

Subtracting a negative number becomes addition:

$$7-(-3)=7+3=10$$

Another example:

$$-7-(-3)=-7+3=-4$$

> When subtracting, change the operation to addition and use the opposite of
> the second number.

### Example With Several Terms

Simplify:

$$-5-(-2)+(-3)$$

First, change subtraction of a negative into addition:

$$-5+2-3$$

Then calculate from left to right:

$$-3-3=-6$$

## 4. Multiplying Positive and Negative Numbers

For multiplication, the signs determine whether the result is positive or
negative.

### Same Signs Give a Positive Result

$$(+)(+)=+$$

$$(-)(-)=+$$

Examples:

$$4\times3=12$$

$$(-4)\times(-3)=12$$

### Different Signs Give a Negative Result

$$(+)(-)=-$$

$$(-)(+)=-$$

Examples:

$$4\times(-3)=-12$$

$$(-4)\times3=-12$$

### Multiplying More Than Two Numbers

- An even number of negative factors gives a positive result.
- An odd number of negative factors gives a negative result.

Two negative factors:

$$(-2)(-3)(4)=24$$

Three negative factors:

$$(-2)(-3)(-4)=-24$$

The result depends on the number of **negative signs**, not whether the values
are odd or even numbers.

---

## 5. Dividing Positive and Negative Numbers

Division uses the same sign rules as multiplication.

### Same Signs Give a Positive Result

$$\frac{+8}{+4}=+2$$

$$\frac{-16}{-2}=+8$$

Examples:

$$12\div3=4$$

$$(-12)\div(-3)=4$$

### Different Signs Give a Negative Result

$$\frac{+9}{-3}=-3$$

$$\frac{-24}{+6}=-4$$

Examples:

$$12\div(-3)=-4$$

$$(-12)\div3=-4$$

> Division by zero is undefined. A number must never be divided by zero.

## Sign Rules Summary

### Addition

| First number | Second number | Rule | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Positive | Positive | Add the magnitudes | Positive |
| Negative | Negative | Add the magnitudes | Negative |
| Positive | Negative | Subtract the magnitudes | Sign of the larger magnitude, or zero if equal |
| Negative | Positive | Subtract the magnitudes | Sign of the larger magnitude, or zero if equal |

### Subtraction

| First number | Number being subtracted | Rewrite as addition | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Positive | Positive | Positive + Negative | Depends on the larger magnitude, or zero if equal |
| Positive | Negative | Positive + Positive | Positive |
| Negative | Positive | Negative + Negative | Negative |
| Negative | Negative | Negative + Positive | Depends on the larger magnitude, or zero if equal |

For subtraction, reverse the sign of the number being subtracted and then use
the addition rules:

$$a-b=a+(-b)$$

### Multiplication and Division

| First sign | Second sign | Result |
|---       |---       |---       |
| Positive | Positive | Positive |
| Negative | Negative | Positive |
| Positive | Negative | Negative |
| Negative | Positive | Negative |

Memory rule:

> Same signs give a positive result. Different signs give a negative result.

This memory rule applies to **multiplication and division**. Addition and
subtraction use the rules explained in their own sections.

---

## Common Mistakes

- Do not use the multiplication sign rule when adding numbers.
- When subtracting, reverse only the sign of the number being subtracted.
- Use brackets to make negative values clear, for example (7-(-3)).
- Remember that subtracting a negative number becomes addition.
- For mixed addition, keep the sign of the number with the larger magnitude.
- Never divide by zero.
