# HCF and LCM

## What HCF Means

**HCF** stands for **Highest Common Factor**.

It is the largest whole number that divides exactly into every number in a group.

In some books and calculators, HCF is called **GCF** or **GCD**:

- **GCF** — Greatest Common Factor
- **GCD** — Greatest Common Divisor
- **HCF**, **GCF**, and **GCD** mean the same thing.

## HCF Prime-Factor Rule

When using prime factorisation:

1. Select only the prime factors that appear in **every number**.
2. When a prime appears more than once, select its **lowest power**.

### HCF Memory Shortcut

> **HCF = common primes with the lowest powers**

### Example

Find the HCF of \(12\) and \(18\).

$$
12 = 2^2 \times 3
$$

$$
18 = 2 \times 3^2
$$

The prime factors common to both numbers are (2) and (3).

Choose their lowest powers:

$$
2^1 \times 3^1
$$

Therefore:

$$
\text{HCF}(12,18)=6
$$

## Why We Need HCF

HCF is used when we need to:

- simplify fractions;
- divide quantities into the largest equal groups;
- find the largest equal size that fits several measurements;
- factorise algebraic expressions;
- simplify ratios.

### Fraction Example

Simplify:

$$
\frac{18}{24}
$$

The HCF of \(18\) and \(24\) is \(6\).

Divide the numerator and denominator by \(6\):

$$
\frac{18 \div 6}{24 \div 6}=\frac{3}{4}
$$

### Real-World Example

Suppose we have:

- \(18\) red components
- \(24\) blue components

We want to make the greatest possible number of identical component packs.

$$
\text{HCF}(18,24)=6
$$

Therefore, we can make \(6\) identical packs.

Each pack contains:

$$
18 \div 6=3
$$

red components and:

$$
24 \div 6=4
$$

blue components.

## LCM

## What LCM Means

**LCM** stands for **Lowest Common Multiple**.

It is the smallest positive number that is a multiple of every number in a group.

A multiple is produced by multiplying a number by a whole number.

For example, multiples of \(4\) are:

$$
4,\ 8,\ 12,\ 16,\ 20,\ 24,\ldots
$$

## LCM Prime-Factor Rule

When using prime factorisation:

1. Include every prime factor that appears in any of the numbers.
2. When a prime appears more than once, select its **highest power**.

### LCM Memory Shortcut

> **LCM = all required primes with the highest powers**

### Example

Find the LCM of \(12\) and \(18\).

$$
12 = 2^2 \times 3
$$

$$
18 = 2 \times 3^2
$$

The prime factors present are \(2\) and \(3\).

Choose their highest powers:

$$
2^2 \times 3^2
$$

Therefore:

$$
\text{LCM}(12,18) = 4 \times 9 = 36
$$

## Why We Need LCM

LCM is used when we need to:

- add or subtract fractions with different denominators;
- find when repeating events occur together;
- find a common cycle or timing interval;
- find the smallest quantity divisible by several numbers;
- find a common denominator.

### Fraction Example

Add:

$$
\frac{1}{6}+\frac{1}{8}
$$

Find the LCM of the denominators:

$$
\text{LCM}(6,8)=24
$$

Convert both fractions:

$$
\frac{1}{6}=\frac{4}{24}
$$

$$
\frac{1}{8}=\frac{3}{24}
$$

Now add:

$$
\frac{4}{24}+\frac{3}{24} = \frac{7}{24}
$$

### Real-World Timing Example

One machine completes a cycle every \(6\) minutes.

Another machine completes a cycle every \(8\) minutes.

Their cycles will finish together after:

$$
\text{LCM}(6,8)=24
$$

Therefore, both machines complete a cycle together every \(24\) minutes.

## HCF and LCM Comparison

## HCF

- Full name: **Highest Common Factor**
- Select only factors found in every number.
- Use the lowest power of each common prime.
- The answer divides exactly into all the original numbers.
- Commonly used for simplification and equal grouping.

## LCM

- Full name: **Lowest Common Multiple**
- Select every prime factor needed by any number.
- Use the highest power of each prime.
- The answer is divisible by all the original numbers.
- Commonly used for fractions, timing and repeating cycles.

## Final Memory Rule

$$
\boxed{\text{HCF = common primes, lowest powers}}
$$

$$
\boxed{\text{LCM = all required primes, highest powers}}
$$

## Important Terminology

Do not confuse these words:

- **Factor:** divides exactly into a number.
- **Multiple:** obtained by multiplying a number.
- **Fraction:** represents part of a whole.
- **Prime factor:** a prime number used to build another number by multiplication.
